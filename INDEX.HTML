Simple Daily Fat Loss Plan

(For Busy Students in Jharkhand, India)

Goal: Lose 6 kg this summer, retain muscle, and reduce belly fat.

1. Morning Yoga (Empty Stomach – 6:30 AM)

Total Time: 20–25 minutes

Routine:

  • Surya Namaskar (Sun Salutations):
    - How Many? 5 rounds (1 round = 12 steps).
    - Time: 10 minutes.
  • Core Poses:
    1. Boat Pose (Navasana):
      - Sit, lift legs, balance on hips, arms forward.
      - Hold 20 seconds → Rest 10 seconds → Repeat 3 times.
    2. Plank Pose:
      - Hold your body straight like a table.
      - Hold 30 seconds → Rest 10 seconds → Repeat 3 times.
    3. Twisting Pose (Ardha Matsyendrasana):
      - Sit, twist spine to one side.
      - Hold 15 seconds per side → Repeat 2 times each side.

2. Evening Bodyweight Workout (8:00 PM)

Total Time: 20–25 minutes
Routine (5 Days/Week):

  • Warm-Up (2 minutes):
    - Spot jogging (run in place) or jumping jacks.
  • Exercises:
    1. Push-Ups:
      - Do 10 push-ups → Rest 20 seconds → Repeat 3 times (Total = 30 push-ups).
    2. Squats:
      - Do 15 squats → Rest 20 seconds → Repeat 3 times (Total = 45 squats).
    3. Plank Shoulder Taps:
      - In plank position, tap left shoulder with right hand, then right shoulder with left hand.
      - 10 taps per side → Rest 20 seconds → Repeat 2 times.
    4. Mountain Climbers:
      - Run knees toward chest while in plank.
      - 30 seconds fast → Rest 20 seconds → Repeat 2 times.

Key Terms Simplified

  • Reps (Repetitions): How many times you do an exercise in a row (e.g., 10 push-ups).
  • Sets: Groups of reps with rest in between (e.g., 3 sets of 10 push-ups).
  • Hold Time: How long you stay in a yoga pose (e.g., Boat Pose for 20 seconds).

Food & Lifestyle Tips

  2. Timing:
    - Breakfast by 10 AM (after yoga).
    - Dinner by 8 PM (after evening workout).
  3. Avoid: Sugar, maida (refined flour), fried snacks.

Morning Yoga Routine (Empty Stomach)

(Focus: Core strength, flexibility, fat burn)

1. Surya Namaskar (Sun Salutation)

Steps (1 Round = 12 Steps):

  1. Pranamasana (Prayer Pose): Stand straight, hands in prayer position.
  2. Hasta Uttanasana (Raised Arms Pose): Inhale, arch back, arms overhead.
  3. Uttanasana (Forward Fold): Exhale, bend forward, touch toes.
  4. Ashwa Sanchalanasana (Lunge): Inhale, step right leg back, left knee bent.
  5. Plank Pose: Hold body straight (like a push-up position).
  6. Ashtanga Namaskar (Eight-Limbed Pose): Lower knees, chest, and chin to floor.
  7. Bhujangasana (Cobra Pose): Inhale, lift chest, keep elbows slightly bent.
  8. Adho Mukha Svanasana (Downward Dog): Exhale, lift hips, form an inverted “V.”
  9. Ashwa Sanchalanasana (Lunge): Inhale, step right foot forward.
  10. Uttanasana (Forward Fold): Exhale, bring left foot forward, bend down.
  11. Hasta Uttanasana (Raised Arms Pose): Inhale, rise up, arch back.
  12. Pranamasana (Prayer Pose): Exhale, return to starting position.

Common Mistakes to Avoid:

  • Rushing through poses → Focus on slow, deep breathing.
  • Arching lower back too much in Cobra Pose → Keep elbows slightly bent, shoulders relaxed.
  • Letting hips sag in Plank → Engage core and glutes to keep body straight.

2. Boat Pose (Navasana)

How to Do It:

  1. Sit on the floor, knees bent, feet flat.
  2. Lean back slightly, lift feet off the floor (knees bent at first).
  3. Straighten legs to form a “V” shape (if possible).
  4. Extend arms forward, parallel to the floor.
  5. Hold for 20 seconds (beginner) → 60 seconds (advanced).

Common Mistakes:

  • Rounding the back → Keep spine straight, chest lifted.
  • Holding breath → Breathe deeply.

3. Plank Pose (Phalakasana)

How to Do It:

  1. Start on all fours, wrists under shoulders.
  2. Step feet back, body straight from head to heels.
  3. Engage core, glutes, and thighs.
  4. Hold for 30 seconds (beginner) → 2 minutes (advanced).

Common Mistakes:

  • Sagging hips → Squeeze glutes and core.
  • Looking up → Neutral neck (gaze at the floor).

4. Cobra Pose (Bhujangasana)

How to Do It:

  1. Lie on your stomach, palms under shoulders.
  2. Press palms down, lift chest off the floor (keep hips grounded).
  3. Keep elbows slightly bent, shoulders away from ears.
  4. Hold for 15–30 seconds.

Common Mistakes:

  • Overarching the neck → Keep gaze forward, not upward.
  • Using only arms → Use back muscles to lift.

5. Seated Twist (Ardha Matsyendrasana)

How to Do It:

  1. Sit with legs straight.
  2. Bend right knee, place right foot outside left thigh.
  3. Twist torso to the right, left elbow outside right knee.
  4. Hold for 15–30 seconds per side.

Common Mistakes:

  • Forcing the twist → Twist from the base of the spine, not shoulders.
  • Collapsing chest → Keep spine tall.

Evening Bodyweight Workout

(Focus: Muscle retention, fat burn)

1. Push-Ups

How to Do It:

  1. Start in plank position (hands under shoulders).
  2. Lower chest toward the floor, elbows at 45° angle.
  3. Push back up to plank.

Common Mistakes:

  • Flaring elbows out → Keep elbows close to ribs.
  • Dropping hips → Engage core and glutes.

Modification:

  • Knee Push-Ups: Do the same motion with knees on the floor.

2. Squats

How to Do It:

  1. Stand with feet shoulder-width apart.
  2. Push hips back, bend knees, lower into a squat (like sitting on a chair).
  3. Keep chest up, knees behind toes.
  4. Push through heels to stand up.

Common Mistakes:

  • Knees caving in → Push knees outward.
  • Rounding back → Keep spine neutral.

3. Plank Shoulder Taps

How to Do It:

  1. Start in plank position.
  2. Tap left shoulder with right hand, then right shoulder with left hand.
  3. Keep hips stable (avoid rocking side to side).

Common Mistakes:

  • Shifting weight → Move slowly, engage core.

4. Mountain Climbers

How to Do It:

  1. Start in plank position.
  2. Drive right knee toward chest, then switch legs quickly.
  3. Keep hips low and core tight.

Common Mistakes:

  • Lifting hips → Imagine a glass of water on your lower back—don’t spill it!

General Safety Tips

  1. Warm-Up: Always do 2–3 minutes of light jogging or arm circles.
  2. Form Over Speed: Move slowly and controlled.
  3. Stop If Painful: Sharp pain = STOP. Mild muscle burn is normal.
  4. Breathe: Exhale during effort (e.g., pushing up in a push-up), inhale during relaxation.
